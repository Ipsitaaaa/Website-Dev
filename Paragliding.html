PARAGLIDING IN BIR BILLING

"The heavens declare the glory of God; the skies proclaim the work of his hands."

Strike it off your bucket list today!

We are offering one of the best paragliding experiences here in Himachal Pradesh. Soar through the air in the Paragliding Capital of India.
Yes! Not anywhere else but in your own country now!
Bir Billing, is heaven for paragliding adventure. Navigating in the air at an altitude where all you can feel is the wind gushing by and looking down on the magnificent picturesque is an experience beyond words.
Feel safe and well guided on reaching Bir Billing, as we have experienced pilots waiting for you. Enjoy a thrilling take off from 2,400 meters in the glade on the highest point of an edge at Billing, glide over the Dhauladhar ranges, lush green fields, the beautiful water streams, and nature's bounty.
This was “First Paragliding World Cup of India.” This event happened in the month of October, 2015.” During world cup 150 top ranked paragliding pilots of all over the world participating along with 500 free flying pilots. This was a great opportunity for many local paragliding pilots to show their skills to world.
Bir Billing provides the option of Paragliding, Trekking to Himachali tribal villages, Mountain Biking, Angling and Camping.
Bir is mainly a Buddhist town and houses a monastery and a Tibetan handicraft center.

Paragliding Info

  • Region: Himachal Pradesh

  • Max Altitude:8,200 ft(approx.)

  • Duration:25-30 mins

  • Grade: Moderate to Difficult

  • Suitable For: 18-45 years

  • Experience: Beginners + Experienced

  • Best Season: March-November

CAUTION

  • Patients with Heart Problem, High blood pressure, Asthma, Epilepsy are not fit for these missions.

  • Avoid consuming heavy food or alcoholic beverages before and after the activity.

  • This activity is not recommended for women who are more than 3months pregnant, anyone that has had major surgery, or has a broken leg/arm, has chronic back or neck pains, etc.

  • Carry Extra pair of clothes.Carrying sharp objects, lighter, knife etc is strictly prohibited.
