TREKKING(ROOPKUND TREK)

Not every puzzle is intended to be solved. Some are in place to test your limits. Others are, in fact, not puzzles at all ... -  Vera Nazarian

Are you ready to explore one of the most mysterious lakes in India?

Yeh Jawani Hai Deewani glamorized Trekking and created a mandatory trekking expedition to all of our wishlists!

Well, as we all know India has one of the best terrains in the world. Roopkund, a picturesque tourist destination located amidst the lap of the benevolent mountains, namely at the base of two Himalayan peaks, Trishul and Nandghunti is one of the most famous treks in India. Besides, it has a mystery of its own.Roopkund Lake steals the spotlight as you can find skeletons by the bank of the lake which is believed to belong to Paleolithic age. The adrenaline pumping trek to Roopkund starts from Lohajung, a tiny pass seated at 16,499 feet above sea level in the Garhwal Himalayas. The alpine meadows offer breath-taking view and the snowclad mountains giving an enigmatic aura that attracts tourists from all around the world. During the trek, you can see how hard the people must work there. Also, get an insight of the Garhwali Pahari Culture. As you advance on this trek, you would find lustrous green grassy lands surrounded with welcoming, pleasant breeze that will sooth your senses. There you can camp at night and enjoy the explicit scenic beauty from the top.

Trek Info

  • Region: Uttarakhand
    Max Altitude:15,696 ft(approx.)

  • Distance:53 km
    Duration:8 – 9 days

  • Grade: Moderate to Difficult

  • Suitable For: 14+ years

  • Experience: Beginners + Experienced

  • Base Camp: Lohajung/Wan

  • Best Season: Summer (April-June), Autumn(August-November)

  • Rail Head: Kathgodam/Lalgola

Itinerary

  • Day 1: Kathgodam to Lohajung (by car or bus)

  • Day 2: Lohajung to Didna Village (trekking starts)

  • Day 3: Didna Village to Ali Bugyal

  • Day 4: Ali Bugyal to PatarNachauni via BedniBugyal

  • Day 5: PatarNachauni to Bhagwabasa via Kalu Vinayak

  • Day 6: Bhagwabasa to Roopkund and back to BedniBugyal

  • Day 7: PatarNachauni to Lohajung via Bedni and Wan

  • Day 8: Lohajung to Kathgodam

CAUTION

  • Patients with Heart Problem, High blood pressure, Asthma, Epilepsy are not fit for these missions.

  • Travelling in monsoon should be avoided due to high risks of landslides and roadblocks.

  • Weather is unpredictable so carry raincoat, extra pair of clothing etc

  • Avoid trekking alone. Give proper information about your trek to your family.

  • Climb from BedniBugyal to Roopkund gets steep so climber must be sufficiently fit and should have an experience of at least one high altitudes trek.

  • Not suitable for people who have undergone recent surgery

Nanda Devi Yatra

The Nanda Devi Raj Jat Yatra is held in every 12 years. It starts from Nautivillage near Karnaprayag to Homkund,nearRoopkund. It is a pilgrimage dedicated to Goddess Nanda Devi. Many people take part in this from all over the Garhwal and Kumaon region in Uttarakhand. It is said by the locals that once in every twelve years a mountain goat is born with four horns in one of the families in the Garhwal region by the blessing of Goddess Nanda Devi. During this Yatra the goat is also taken and on reaching Homkund, several ceremonies are done worshipping Nanda Devi. Then, the goat is left there. It is believed that the goat wanders around the terrain and reaches Kailash.

Mythology

According to Hindu Mythology, when Lord Shiva and Goddess Parvati were heading towards Kailash after their marriage ceremony, goddess Parvati wanted to drink water. Then, Shiva created a ‘kund’ or lake with the help of his Trishul. When goddess Parvati leaned forward to drink water from the lake, she saw her reflection and was bewitched by it. Hence, the name Roopkund. While,it is believed by the locals that once during the Nanda Devi Yatra,the King of Kanauj had set out for this Yatra with his people. During this time, his wife Rani Balampa, was pregnant and insisted to join them for this pilgrimage. In the course, the queen had to deliver a baby (which is considered unclean) which infuriated the Goddess. Their entourage disregarding the pious customs enraged the goddess and she showed her wrath in the form of a severe hailstorm. It is said that hailstones or ‘shilas’ fell like meteors all over the place as a punishment for their disregard. After years of research on the skeletons found in Roopkund, it is believed that those hailstones hit the pilgrims at their head leading to sudden death. Though the exact number of death remains a mystery but it is believed that over the years number of skeletons have perished. The icy cold weather is the only reason that the scientists were able to get skeletons for their research. There are many skeletons in the Roopkund Lake as well which can be seen floating by the tourists during autumn.
